Dear friend, 

Thanks for clicking in. Glad to see you here! 

This is the first public newsletter delivered to you after the redesign of jimmyzhang.org in April 2026. I wrote about it [here](https://jimmyzhang.org/newsletter/1-year-old). 

The month started with the celebration of last day of high school. I had always somehow rejected the notion of celebrating this event, so I quickly packed up and went directly back home to China for a three week break before I come back for my IB exam. 

At home, I first shared [Delphi](https://delphi-web.netlify.app), an AI chatbot better at listening, asking questions, and helping you think clearly. For myself, it generates quite a surprising level of emotional intelligence and depth during the first few times using. 

Staying home is just great for deep focus. I also rediscovered the analog notebook as a powerful tool for thinking — [[computer-as-tool]]. 

I started actually thinking about the academic pathway that I want to pursue at University of Michigan, and landed on the intersections of fields like Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning, Game Theory, and mechanism design. 

I briefly visited Hangzhou, went to Xixi National Wetland Park and watched the Most Memorable Is Hangzhou performance. I also spent about 6 hours playing [The Stanley Parable](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1703340/The_Stanley_Parable_Ultra_Deluxe/) — a truly fantastic and piece of game that is just "meta" at a different level. 

Most of the month went into re-designing https://jimmyzhang.org with Claude Code — which, at $20/month, replaced both my Obsidian Publish and Squarespace subscriptions. Reflections here: [[ai-eats-saas]] and [[ai-success]]. 

Finally, I finished reading half of the novel [一句顶一万句 (Someone to Talk To)](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/35398341-someone-to-talk-to) by Zhenyun Liu (刘震云) — a long-overdue return to Chinese literature. The novel's portrayal of complex human relationships and the most authentic conditions of living is genuinely astonishing. 

Just before my departure, a small moment reminded me of art's power — [[flower-by-lake]].

Back in the Netherlands, I sat my IB Physics exam. Reviewing for it sparked some thoughts — [[ib-physics]]. Meanwhile, I shipped [Cahier](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=C-CrTilSquI), a small app that I made for myself to learn french. I finished the book [[Finite and Infinite Games]], which deepened how I think about life and the roles we play in it.

With that concludes April 2026. 

Hope you enjoyed this issue. 

Sincerely, 
Jimmy Zhang
