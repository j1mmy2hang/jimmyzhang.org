---
title: "Personal Statement"
---
> I spent 9 years of my life in the national Chinese curriculum, 
> 1 year in UWC Changshu, a prestigious international school, 
> and 2 years in UWC Maastricht, a boarding public-school in the Netherlands. 
> This year, I will go to University of Michigan, Ann Arbor for the next chapter. 

---

Over those last years in China's public school, I developed the ability to feel pain. 

The pain my education left me with. Few ever learn to feel it. Most follow on the beaten path, numb and unfeeling. 

And yes, the ability to feel and think is the source of pain. Through a few inspiring teachers outside the system, and through the books I began to read, I started to question what I did every day. I compared my life to those I encountered elsewhere, and felt the weight of the difference. Sometimes it was overwhelming; I would leave the classroom and go to the rooftop, just to be alone for a while.

Despite the pain, I have no sense of where to go next. The expected path—the top local high school—stood as the only visible future, and I knew nothing beyond it. 

I didn't quite remember why I applied to UWC Changshu. But I did remember that I didn't look to UWC Changshu as an escape, not really. In retrospect, UWC was then just a different path to me. Where it leads to I have absolutely no idea. 

But as long as it's something different and exciting, and can provide any possibility of change. 

It was that confusing state of not wanting to continue school in the original system and not knowing where to go next that led me to UWC Changshu. 

---

The pain softened during the 1 year in UWC Changshu, as it indeed opened up a completely new land for me. There are so many new things to learn about and explore, new people to meet, new life options to explore. It indeed feel like leaving a prison after 9 years. 

The world opened—new people, new ideas, new lives to imagine. It felt like release after years of enclosure.

However, maybe it was a bit too overwhelming that I decided to leave after the first year. It was also partly due to some frustrations as I struggle to explore my personal identity in this community. Believing that I, with no prior advantage and already behind, had no advantage in this system, I tried to "escape" again. 

A friend asked my why did I choose to leave, I said, it's new experience and opportunities elsewhere, why not. I didn't want to admit that I was escaping, I really didn't believe that. 

However, after all these year, "escape" seemed to be truly the central theme throughout my early life, especially after I went to UWC Maastricht in the Netherlands. 

---

So I transferred to UWC Maastricht hoping for a better environment where I can have some news experiences while developing myself and catching up on the nine years missed. 

However, it was a huge disappointment. 

Not only that it felt much more like a public school again with strict rules, rigid beauracracy, and lots of inflexibilities, the community life compared to UWC Changshu was also greatly under-developed.

In UWC Changshu it feels like we are a big community living on an island. In Maastricht we also claim to be on an island (surrounded not by a lake, but a narrow moat), but we feel like tenants who happen to need to live here, managed by a bossy landlord. The school has day students who treat here not as their home but as, well, a school. It is in this weird mixture of place that I live in. 

My frustration also came from the fact that my rate of learning is growing exponentially faster and faster (since the first days I have got my hands on my laptop), and I quickly outpaced everything I am supposed to learn in the IB program. 

I struggled to spend 8 hours of my life every day having to go through a sets of rooms from which I learn nothing from. 

I struggled, suffered, and thought about leaving again, but is stopped by the uncertain future of leaving school, and at the end of the day realizing that I am nobody without the tagline of being a student here and outside the system. 

Yes, turned out I can call myself an agentic self-directed learner or whatever, but I am nobody without this institution. I am nobody had I chose to stay in China, and I am nobody had I leave. 

Nobody is forcing me to stay, yet there is no choice. 

Discouraged, I learned to suppress my pain and went thought the two years. I promised myself to never make myself suffer again in my life and to never forget the pain. I wrote a poem called Remember, hoping that years later I wouldn't forget what I had been through. 

> Remember the struggle,
> remember the pain,
> remember what you've lost that you hope to regain. 
> 
> Remember your love, 
> remember your hate, 
> remember what makes you yourself today.

---

My result for university application turned out to be slightly disappointing, partially because I had long held fantasy that I deserve something better and is exponentially smarter than people around me. I also suspect it was because I wrote just a bit too many negative aspects about my education experiences and how I didn't need school in my personal statement. 

In retrospect it was indeed quite arrogant and ignorant of how the game works. 

I ended up only having one offer from University of Michigan and Ann Arbor, which I have no option but to commit to. I didn't choose it, it chose me. 

I still have no clear idea of academic path, nor anything I do that gives me a clear edge that I can showcase to the world. I still lack experience dealing with people, whom I used to consider to be obstacles and inefficiencies in my pursuit of greatness. 

But now, after application, my views are changing. I had a deeper understanding into how all these systems and their game works, and how I can better play them. I realized the world is made up of people and the games they made, so it's better if I can learn to better deal with people and participate in the games, compared to working on my laptop alone. 

---

But it's just a beginning. There are still lots more to experience and learn. 

I convinced myself to start being proud of Umich, to love the big M logo against the naval blue color. I told myself environment doesn't matter that much, and what matters is your change in mindset and what you actually do besides complaining to escape. 

I don't know if I can start anew in UMich, as I imagined before I came to Maastricht. But regardless, I will be open towards whatever the world still have to offer me, because my journey is far from its destination. 

I don't hold hope to transfer to Stanford or elsewhere in second-year. I might try, but for now, the mission is to live my life to the fullest in Ann Arbor. 

And I hope for the first time in my life, Umich is not another escape, but somewhere I truly enjoy to be at.
