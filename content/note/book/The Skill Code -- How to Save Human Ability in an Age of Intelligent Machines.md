## My Comments

This is one of those books that just don't quite click for me. The problem statement of "machines automating tasks so that experts don't need novices and novices can't learn by doing" is somewhat far-fetched and strained. 

Rather, I think the fundamental problem is this -- AI makes human less necessary in the passindg-down of knowledge and skills. Of course in any field, to become the top 1%, interaction with experts, field-leaders, and real-life experiences are still necessary, but the road before that has been made much easier by AI. 

The expert-novice bond is still necessary, but very much less so. AI will enable new powerful ways to the novices to learn -- more effective than through running errands for the expert.
