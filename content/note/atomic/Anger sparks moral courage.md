When it comes to standing up to others’ wrongdoings, anger plays an important role.
The more anger one experiences, the more likely they are to show moral courage.

We often think of anger as a negative emotion linked to aggression, but it turns out that <u>it can also be a force for good.</u>

Anger is a common reaction to wrongdoings, and it provides us with a strong urge to make things right (stronger than other emotions such as empathy). 

> [!summary]
> Anger, rather than empathy, is the **main source of motivation** that drives us to act morally courageous and stand up for what's right.
