<u>On one hand -- the tendency to use rationality and resort to cause and effect</u>

> we have been taught to analyze, to use our rational ability, to trace direct paths from cause to effect, to look at things in small and understandable pieces, to solve problems by acting on or controlling the world around us

[[humans-tend-to-think-in-linearities-and-causations]]

<u>On the other hand -- intuition without analysis based on experience </u>

> long before we were educated in rational analysis, we all dealt with complex systems. We have built up intuitively, without analysis, often without words, a practical understanding of how these systems work, and how to work with them.

e.g. 
A stitch in time saves nine. 
The rich get richer and the poor get poorer. 
Don't put all your eggs in one basket.
