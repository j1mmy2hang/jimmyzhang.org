The internet is supposed to be wonderful places full of genuine voices, constructive conversations, and high quality contents.

The Internet was once hailed as a democratic, decentralized space — a “global village” for sharing knowledge, connecting communities, and empowering individuals. But today, much of our online experience is filtered through black-box algorithms designed not to inform, but to _capture and manipulate our attention_. These algorithms, optimized for engagement at all costs, often distort our perception of reality, polarize discourse, and exploit our psychology for profit.
