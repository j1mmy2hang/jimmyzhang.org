Creating Common Knowledge is a **high stakes act**. 
It is powerful because it forces the situation into a new stake and **removes the exit strategy** -- binary and irreversible. 

If you make a proposition explicitly (creating Common Knowledge), you force the other person to accept the new reality or explicitly reject you. There is no middle ground.

- It forces the world into a specific state.
- Once something is "Out There" (Common Knowledge), you cannot take it back. You cannot "un-ring the bell."

As a result

[[Humans tend to strategically avoid common knowledge and replace with euphemisms and innuendo to navigate relationships]]
