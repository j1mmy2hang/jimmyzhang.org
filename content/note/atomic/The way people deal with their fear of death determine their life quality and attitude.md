> In many cases people push all thoughts of death into the back recesses of the mind while continuing to run their lives and business *at a frantic pace*. Whether people harness this activity in a positive way or runaround like a rat in a maze is an indication of how well they address fears of their inevitable end.

In order to temporarily escape death (the great ultimate source of meaninglessness), we strive to create meaning through work / life. But whether we do this passively (being constantly driven by the fear e.g. workaholism) OR actively (building meaning & creating legacies based on a full & calm comprehension and acknowledgement of death) determines our attitude toward life. 

> [!summary]
> A good attitude toward life is a finding good balance between life and death, between 努力与放弃, between meaning and meaninglessness.
