# Two Worldviews: Creation-Freedom vs Extraction-Distribution

## Core Opposition

**Extraction Worldview**: Wealth is a fixed pie to be seized and divided
**Creation Viewview**: Wealth is generated through innovation and voluntary exchange

## The Extraction-Distribution Paradigm

### Fundamental Assumptions

- Resources are **finite and pre-existing**
- Economics is **zero-sum**: your gain is my loss
- The central question: "Who gets what?"
- Power determines distribution
- Fairness means **equal division**

### Political Implications

- Emphasis on **redistribution mechanisms**
- Regulation as protection from exploitation
- Suspicion of profit and accumulation
- Collective ownership as moral ideal
- Authority must **allocate** resources

### Psychological Roots

- Scarcity mindset
- Tribal resource competition (evolutionary heritage)
- Visible wealth triggers resentment
- Moral intuition: "They took more than their share"

## The Creation-Freedom Paradigm

### Fundamental Assumptions

- Wealth is **generated**, not discovered
- Economics is **positive-sum**: trade creates value
- The central question: "How do we create more?"
- Innovation drives prosperity
- Fairness means **voluntary exchange**

### Political Implications

- Emphasis on **enabling conditions** (property rights, rule of law)
- Freedom as prerequisite for innovation
- Profit as signal and incentive
- Individual initiative as engine
- Authority must **protect** the creative process

### Psychological Roots

- Abundance mindset -- [[scarcity-mindset-vs-abundance-mindset]]
- Builder/maker identity
- Visible wealth inspires emulation
- Moral intuition: "They created something valuable"
