> People with disabilities have a long history of being screened out in schooling—in life in general, for that matter.

> Thomas West, an author who is dyslexic himself and was “not reading until way too late,” has written powerfully about the need to appreciate and recognize different ways of thinking. His mission is much like my own: to help people appreciate different kinds of minds and ensure that they are not screened out by a one-size-fits-all educational system.
