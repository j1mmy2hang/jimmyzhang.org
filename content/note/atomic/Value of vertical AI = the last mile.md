## Opinionated Perspective

Lines of code are the least interesting part of software. Software that works means that somebody sat down and designed a workflow, and the logic of that workflow encapsulated the way that things are done. Software is a stored process. ==It’s not a neutral tool: it’s an opinion for how a group of people should collaborate, encoded in a durable system.== 

[[The moat of software in the age of AI is an opinionated perspective on the solution]]

## Why general model fails

General-purpose AI tools, however strong they get, can’t be opinionated in that way. That’s not a criticism of them: it’s a structural reflection of what they have to do. Anthropic and OpenAI are building for every use-case on earth at once; they can’t be opinionated about the specific preferences for how the KKR credit team likes to structure things. They don’t know, they can’t know, they’ll likely never know: that’s just not their job.
