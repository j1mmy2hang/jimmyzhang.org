the system on paper ≠ the real system at work

> "Many leaders falsely assume that their impact depends on their position in this written system (system on paper), and as a result they do not play the game."

--> Effective leadership requires shifting focus from the static, formal organizational structure ("system on paper") to understanding and engaging with the dynamic, influential network of human relationships and interdependencies ("your system").

System leaders achieve results by working *with* the inherent dynamics of the human system—leveraging clarity, understanding resistance, and amplifying existing momentum—rather than imposing top-down control.
