[[Great startup ideas don't start out looking obvious]]

Believing in and relentlessly searching for "secrets"—valuable, non-obvious truths hidden from conventional wisdom about nature or people—is the essential first step to build innovative businesses that provide unique value  ([[0 to 1 vs 1 to n -- Radical technological creation establishing new monopolies is essential for a better future and superior to merely competing within or expanding existing paradigms through globalization and iteration|0 to 1]]) and escape the [[Competition is a destructive economic reality that erodes profits, stifles innovation, and pushes people toward ruthlessness or death|competition fray]]

> "What important truth do very few people agree with you on?"

A good answer:
Most poeople belive in x, but the truth is the opposite of x. 

> If you can identify a delusional popular belief, you can find what lies hidden behind it: the contrarian truth.
> -- [[Zero to One]]

> Conventional beliefs only ever come to appear arbitrary and wrong in retrospect; whenever one collapses, we call the old belief a bubble.
> -- [[Zero to One]]

## A company is a conspiracy

> "So who do you tell [your secret]? Whoever you need to, and no more. In practice, there’s always a golden mean between telling nobody and telling everybody—and that’s a company. The best entrepreneurs know this: every great business is built around a secret that’s hidden from the outside. A great company is a conspiracy to change the world; when you share your secret, the recipient becomes a fellow conspirator."

> "Great companies have secrets: specific reasons for success that other people don’t see."
