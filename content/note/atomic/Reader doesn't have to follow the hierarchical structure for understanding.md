The structure of the author doesn't matter much to the reader

The structure is just a framework, and it's just the author's framework.
It may make you feel good and organized to follow the created structure, but it doesn't really matter if you really learned that idea in chapter 1 or chapter 3, as long as you understand how that idea connects to the others. 

Readers can perfectly re-create their own framework of understanding based on what they learned and their takeaway (I love this term) -- using linking, tagging, etc.
