A significant portion of the West’s leadership class, from academia to Silicon Valley, has been culturally conditioned to avoid authentic belief and conviction, leading to a hollowed-out public square where moral courage is punished and ineffectual, administrative posturing is rewarded.

> “The problem is that those who say nothing wrong often say nothing much at all. An overly timid engagement with the debates of our time will rob one of the ferocity of feeling that is necessary to move the world.”

The deconstruction of the Western Civilization narrative in American universities, culminating in works like Edward Said's *Orientalism*, was a necessary challenge to an exclusionary canon but ultimately left a cultural void by failing to replace the old narrative with a new, coherent, and aspirational vision of national identity.
