## Principle

> "Only those who will risk going too far can possibly find out how far one can go.”
> -- T.S. Eliot

## Method

To find the edge, you must intentionally go past it.

- **Overshoot, Then Dial Back:** Instead of cautiously inching toward a boundary, you deliberately push beyond what seems reasonable. You add "too much" chocolate to the cookie, make the design element "too" prominent, or push the technical constraint to its breaking point. Only then can you dial it back with the confidence of knowing where the actual limit lies.

- **Treat Everything as an Experiment:** Frame your work as a series of experiments. This mindset lowers the fear of failure. The cost of "ruining" one attempt is a small price to pay for the knowledge gained.

> It's better to overshoot it and then dial it back than to try to come up right to the edge of a line that actually is way further than you thought.
