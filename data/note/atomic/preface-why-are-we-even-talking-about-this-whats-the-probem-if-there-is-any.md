Whenever someone asks me, what is the biggest problem of education that I have observed during my 11 years of schooling in two three schools across two continents, I would say that it's the fact that most people struggle to see and realize the problem. 

Now we have to be very careful here with this statement -- while not being able to see the proble doesn't mean the problem doesn't exist, it is meanwhile extremely important to note that we are not inventing a problem our of thin air here. It is dangerouos to try to fix a problem when there is in fact none -- that's how most good-intended projects fail and the roots of many historical tragedies of humanity. 

Instead, what I meant for education is different. School is such a tradition-bound place that has been normalized for hunreds is not thousands of years. It has become a legal obligation in many countries for parents to send their children to school, and "going to school" has been accepted and first and inevitabel stage of life -- our fleeting existance in this vast universe.

And it's extremely hard for people to question things they believed or normalized. People like to question and challenge the legitmacy of certain companies or insitutions, but few people ever thought about challenging the legitamacy of a school. In fact, both schools and those companies are of the kind -- they are all social institutions performing a certain role to serve the society. Howevere, while the companies market is competitive, volatile, and change-abound, the schools industry is very much less so. Yes, schools compete for the grades of their students and for their ranking, but does that really has anything to do with their roles and responsbilities as socail institutions? What role do they actually play and what responsibilitites do they bear?

These are the questions that I am going to explore with you throughout this publication. By breaking down the concept of education to the very bottom, while  observing it from a distance apart in a macro perspective, I hope to not effect changes, but merely to inspire thinking and reflection. I am not to fabricate problems where none exist, but only in an attempt to point out the problems that so many people fail to see.
