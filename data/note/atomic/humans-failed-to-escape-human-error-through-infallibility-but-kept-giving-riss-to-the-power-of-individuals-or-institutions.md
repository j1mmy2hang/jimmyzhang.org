**Religion & God**

superhuman mechanism free from error
religion -- provide superhuman legitimacy for the social order by connecting to a superhuman and infallible intelligence.

<u>problem</u> -- the need for a god's spokesperson
--> as long as religious institutions like oracular temples were staffed by fallible humans, they too were open to error and corruption -- possible to bypass the humans altogether?

**Holy Books**

The book ensures that many people in many times and places can access the same database

<u>problem</u> -- even if the technology of the book succeeded in limiting changes to the holy words, *the world beyond the book* continued to spin, and it was unclear how to relate old rules to new situations --> the need for *human interpretation*

“Trust the infallible book” turned into “trust the humans who interpret the book.”

The attempt to invest all authority in an infallible superhuman technology led to the rise of a new and extremely powerful human institution—the church.

**Church**

The church sought to lock society inside an echo chamber, allowing the spread only of those books that supported it, and people trusted the church because almost all the books supported it.
