Human beings shouldn’t be used or treated as a mere means. 

> Rational beings can never be treated merely as means to ends; they must always also be treated as ends in themselves, requiring that their own reasoned motives must be equally respected.

> If a person is an end-in-themself it means their inherent value doesn't depend on anything else - it doesn't depend on whether the person is enjoying their life ([[self-worth-is-value-you-assign-to-yourself-for-yourself|自我价值]]?), or making other people's lives better([[value-to-others-is-the-value-you-can-bring-to-others|他人价值]]?). We exist, so we have value. ([source](https://www.bbc.co.uk/ethics/introduction/endinitself.shtml))
