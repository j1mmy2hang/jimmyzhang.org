title: "Personal Statement"
---

I spent 9 years of my life in the national Chinese curriculum. 
Spent 1 year in UWC Changshu, a prestigious international school, 
and 2 years in UWC Maastricht, a boarding public-school in the Netherlands. 

---

Since the last several years of my school in China, I have acquired the ability to feel the pain that my education has inflicted upon me, as I began reading more books and learning after several very inspirational individual teachers outside the system. I began to think and to question what I do every day, compare myself to those that I read or learn about elsewhere, and thus feel great disapointment and pain in the difference in living conditions of me and others. I remember having to leave the classroom once is a while just to be alone for a while in the open-air rooftop. 

The pain ceased during the 1 year in UWC Changshu, as there are so many new things to learn about and explore, as if first time actually living after 9 years of prison. Maybe it's a bit too overwhelming that I decided to leave immediately after, believing that I, with no prior advantage and already behind, have no advantage in this system. 

So I transferred to UWC Maastricht hoping for on one hand more experience and on another hand a better environment for me to develop my character. However, dissapointingly, the education that UWC Maastricht provides turned out to be nearly as bad as the national curriculum in China, only that the curriculum is now IB. It is also because my rate of learning and understanding is growth exponentially faster and faster, which makes me quickly outpace the development of the IB program. I struggled, suffered, and thought about leaving again, but is stopped by the uncertain future of leaving school, and at the end of the day realizing that I am nobody without the tagline of being a student here and outside the system. 

Discouraged, I learned to suppress my pain and went thought the two years. I promised myself to never make myself suffer again in my life and to never forget the pain. I wrote myself a poem called Remember. 

> Remember the struggle,
> remember the pain,
> remember what you've lost that you hope to regain. 
> 
> Remember your love, 
> remember your hate, 
> remember what makes you yourself today.

My result for university application turned out to be slightly disappointing, partially due to my long-held fantasy that I deserve something better and is exponentially smarter than people around me. I ended up only having one offer from University of Michigan and Ann Arbor, which I have committed to. 

I still have no clear idea of academic path, nor anything I do that gives me a clear edge that I can showcase to the world. I still lack experience dealing with people, whom I used to consider to be obstacles and inefficiencies in my pursuit of greatness. 

Now my views have changed, but it's just a beginning. There are still lots more to experience and learn. 

I don't know if I can start anew in UMich, as I imagined before I came to Maastricht. But regardless, I will be open towards whatever the world still have to offer me, because my journey is far from its destination. 

I don't hold great hope to transfer to Stanford or elsewhere in second-year. I will try, but for now, the mission is to live my life to the fullest in Ann Arbor.
